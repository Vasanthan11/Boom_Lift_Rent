When to choose a boom lift over a scissor lift?
  • • When you need both height and horizontal outreach around obstacles.
  • • For access above machinery, pipelines, canopies or lower roofs.
  • • For façade work where you cannot place the machine directly below the work area.
  • • For complex industrial maintenance needing side reach at different levels.

Types of boom lifts we rent

We provide different boom lift configurations so you can match the machine to your working height, outreach, and site conditions.

Articulated boom lifts
Knuckle booms for tight access

Ideal where you need to go "up-and-over" obstacles like pipes, ducts, mezzanines or roofs. Great for industrial, MEP and retrofit projects.

Common heights: 30 ft – 60 ft
Telescopic boom lifts
Straight booms for long reach

Used where you need maximum horizontal reach, such as large open sites, yards, hangars, ports or infrastructure works.

Common heights: 40 ft – 80 ft
Electric / hybrid booms
Low emission for indoor / sensitive sites

Suitable for enclosed spaces like factories, malls and plants where noise and fumes must be minimal, while still needing boom outreach.

Options: battery, bi-energy, hybrid

Where boom lifts are commonly used

Boom lifts are chosen when you need flexibility, horizontal outreach and multiple work positions from the same machine setup.

Façade & exterior finishing Cladding, glazing, painting, waterproofing, signage and external repairs on high buildings and structures.
Industrial & plant maintenance Access over equipment, tanks, pipelines and structures during planned shutdowns or routine maintenance.
Infrastructure & utilities Bridges, flyovers, airports, refineries, power plants and utility structures where reach and flexibility are critical.
Warehouse & logistics hubs High-bay racking, lighting, fire lines and system installation in large distribution centres.
Why contractors pick boom lifts
One machine – many positions

With a boom lift, you can reach several working points without moving the base machine every time. This saves setup time and increases productive hours on site.

Typical boom lift height & reach bands

The exact specification depends on model and manufacturer, but the bands below will give you a quick sense of what is commonly available.

Working height band Max horizontal reach* Common applications
16 – 30 ft Up to ~6 m Indoor maintenance, small factories, mezzanine access.
30 – 45 ft Up to ~8–10 m Commercial buildings, warehouses, service yards.
45 – 60 ft Up to ~12–14 m Industrial plants, malls, infrastructure mid-level works.
60 – 80 ft + Beyond 15 m Tall structures, large infrastructure and specialised sites.

*Exact reach depends on individual boom lift model – please confirm when you request a quote.
